<p><p>DNA transfection reagents are categorized into biological methods and physical/chemical methods based on their mechanisms for delivering DNA into cells. Biological methods include the use of viral vectors and lipid-based systems to facilitate cellular uptake. In contrast, physical/chemical methods involve techniques like electroporation or microinjection, which use electrical pulses or direct mechanical force. Each method varies in efficiency, safety, and applicability, catering to different research needs and cell types in fields like gene therapy and molecular biology.</p></p>
