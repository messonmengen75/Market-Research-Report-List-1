<p><p>Isothermal Nucleic Acid Amplification Technology (INAAT) is a molecular biology technique used to amplify nucleic acids under constant temperature conditions. Unlike traditional PCR, which requires thermal cycling, INAAT leverages isothermal conditions to enhance the efficiency and speed of nucleic acid amplification. This technology facilitates rapid diagnostics and has gained traction in various applications, including infectious disease detection, genetic testing, and food safety.</p><p>The INAAT market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing demand for rapid diagnostic solutions and the rising incidence of infectious diseases. The ease of use and the reduced requirement for specialized equipment further contribute to its adoption in both laboratory and point-of-care settings. Current trends include the development of more sensitive and specific amplification techniques, integration with microfluidics for automated systems, and advancements in mobile diagnostic platforms. </p><p>As healthcare systems worldwide prioritize timely and accurate diagnostics, the INAAT market is projected to expand, with an expected CAGR of 9.6% during the forecast period.
