<p><strong>The Surgical 3D Printing Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Stereolithography, SLA (Liquid Based)</li><li>Fused Deposition Modeling, FDM (Solid Based)</li><li>Selective Laser Sintering, SLS ( Powder Based)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Surgical 3D Printing market encompasses several key technologies. Stereolithography (SLA) uses liquid resin cured by lasers to create precise models, ideal for complex structures. Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) involves layering thermoplastic materials, offering durability and affordability for prosthetics. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) utilizes powdered materials sintered together by lasers, enabling intricate designs and strong parts. Each method serves specific surgical needs, enhancing preoperative planning, patient-specific implants, and medical device prototyping, thereby advancing surgical outcomes and personalized medicine.</p></p>
