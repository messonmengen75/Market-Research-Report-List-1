<p><p>Hydrolyzed Animal Protein (HAP) refers to proteins derived from animal sources that have been broken down into smaller peptides or amino acids through hydrolysis. The market encompasses two main types: enzymatic hydrolysis and acid hydrolysis. Enzymatic hydrolysis involves using specific enzymes to achieve protein breakdown, resulting in high-quality, easily digestible products, often used in food and nutritional supplements. Acid hydrolyzed animal protein relies on strong acids for protein degradation, typically producing lower-quality proteins, commonly utilized in industrial applications.</p></p>
