<p><p>Supplementary Cementing Materials (SCMs) are materials used in concrete production to enhance its properties, improve sustainability, and reduce the environmental impact of traditional cement. Common SCMs include fly ash, slag, silica fume, and natural pozzolans, which serve as partial replacements for Portland cement. The versatility of SCMs allows for improved workability, durability, and resistance to chemical attacks in concrete structures.</p><p>The current market for SCMs is on an upward trajectory, driven by increasing construction activities and a growing emphasis on sustainable building practices. The rise in infrastructure projects, particularly in emerging economies, and stringent regulations on carbon emissions are propelling the demand for eco-friendly alternatives in concrete production.
