<p><p>The veterinary anthrax vaccines market comprises two primary types: cell-free protective antigen (PA) vaccines and live vaccines. Cell-free PA vaccines utilize non-infectious components of the bacterium, providing a safer method for immunization, particularly in sensitive populations. In contrast, live vaccines contain attenuated strains of the anthrax bacterium, offering robust immune responses but requiring careful handling due to potential safety concerns. Both types aim to protect livestock and wildlife from anthrax outbreaks, ensuring animal health and food safety.</p></p>
